Magic in Combat

Use this after you know your basic combat loop and your magic tradition.

1

Buff before initiative when fiction allows it

MYT combat actions; AiG Folk Magic p.63-68

Many spells are strongest when cast before steel is drawn. If the party is kicking in a door, agree on the buff routine first.

  • Folk Magic: Bladesharp, Protection, Mobility, Fanaticism.
  • Theism: Shield, Truesword, cult-specific protections.
  • Animism: ready a ward or healing spirit.
  • Sorcery: pre-shape long-duration protective spells if time and MP allow.
2

Casting spends the same attention as fighting

MYT p.92-93; Magic Path stage 2

Casting in combat competes with attacking, parrying, and movement. A caster who spends an Action Point may have fewer defenses later in the cycle.

Table check: before you cast, say how many AP you have left and who is keeping enemies off you.
3

Spoil Spell makes bodyguards matter

MYT p.97, p.121

If an enemy can hit you while you are casting, they may be able to ruin the spell. Casters need space, cover, or allies.

  • Warriors can hold enemies away from casters.
  • Casters can hold magic or prepare before opening the fight.
  • Everyone should know who protects the shaman, priest, or sorcerer.
4

Spirits change the battlefield

MYT Animism p.131-154; ADR-0006

A bound spirit is another tactical resource. It can heal, scout, protect, or fight spirits while the physical battle continues.

Daka Fal example: command an ancestor spirit to distract a hostile spirit, then use your own AP for movement or defense.
5

Miracles are scarce; know what you learned

ADR-002; ADR-0007; NfP cult lists

Theist characters do not carry every possible miracle ready to use. Your Initiate Devotional Pool is POW/2, so arrive with your learned/selected miracles already recorded.

  • Record each miracle's category, rune, cost, and recovery rule; a balanced learned set is usually safer than all offense.
  • Associated-cult magic is one-time use until you return to the associated cult and resacrifice; mark it before the session.
6

Sorcery rewards pre-built spell profiles

MYT p.162-178; AiG p.60

Write each sorcery spell as at least two profiles: fast/minimal and slow/full. This prevents rule lookup during combat.

Example: Palsy fast profile for a single location now; shaped profile for range, duration, or magnitude when you have time.
7

Party script: say what happens before the dice

MYT p.74-106; ADR-0006

Before a dangerous scene, agree on roles: point fighter, bodyguard, healer, spirit answer, counter-magic answer, retreat signal.
