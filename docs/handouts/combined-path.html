Magic in Combat

Read this after you understand the basic combat loop and your own magic tradition.

1

Buff before initiative when the fiction allows it

Adventures in Glorantha p.63-68

Many spells are strongest before steel is drawn. If the party is about to enter danger, agree on the buff routine before the first roll.

  • Folk Magic examples: Bladesharp, Protection, Mobility, Fanaticism.
  • Theist examples: Shield, Truesword, and cult-specific protection miracles.
  • Animist examples: ready a healing, warding, or scouting spirit.
  • Sorcery example: prepare a shaped defensive spell when time allows.
2

Casting spends the same attention as fighting

Mythras Core p.92-93

In combat, casting competes with attacking, parrying, moving, and other urgent actions. A caster who spends an Action Point may have fewer defenses later in the cycle.

Table check: before you cast, say how many Action Points you have left and who is keeping enemies away from you.
3

Interruptions are dangerous

Mythras Core p.97 and p.121

If an enemy can hit you while you are casting, the spell may fail or be spoiled. Casters need space, cover, or allies.

  • Warriors can hold enemies away from casters.
  • Casters should avoid starting long actions while exposed.
  • The party should know who protects the priest, shaman, or sorcerer.
4

Spirits are tactical allies

Mythras Core p.131-154

A bound spirit can heal, scout, protect, or fight spirits while the physical battle continues. Know what each spirit does before initiative.

Example: command an ancestor spirit to distract a hostile spirit, then use your own Action Point for movement or defense.
5

Miracles are powerful but limited

Adventures in Glorantha p.69-122

Theist characters should arrive with selected miracles already recorded. Your Devotional Pool is limited, and some miracle categories need a specific place or cult contact to recover.

  • Write each miracle's rune, cost, duration, and recovery rule.
  • Mark associated cult miracles clearly because they are one-use until recovered through the associated cult.
6

Sorcery rewards pre-built spell profiles

Mythras Core p.162-178

Write each sorcery spell as at least two profiles: fast/minimal and slow/shaped. This prevents rule lookup during combat.

Example: a fast spell for one target now; a shaped version for range, duration, or stronger effect when there is time.
7

Agree on party roles before danger

Mythras Core p.74-106

Before a dangerous scene, agree on point fighter, bodyguard, healer, spirit answer, counter-magic answer, and retreat signal.
