The Magic Path

All five Mythras magic systems — learn the foundations, then branch into your tradition

1

Magic Points & Casting Basics

Core p.116–121 · AiG p.59–62

All Systems Every character has Magic Points = POW. These fuel all magic. They regenerate naturally.

Universal Casting Rules:
  • Casting takes 1 Turn (your action for the round) unless stated otherwise
  • Casting costs Magic Points (varies by system)
  • A Critical success = zero MP cost. A Fumble = lose 1d3 MP, spell fails.
  • You can be interrupted mid-cast (Spoil Spell special effect)
In AiG, all casting skills are replaced by Rune Affinities. You don't roll "Folk Magic" — you roll your relevant Rune (e.g., Fire Rune to cast Ignite).
2

Folk Magic

Core p.122–130 · AiG p.63–68

Folk Magic Simple, universal magic available to everyone. The "cantrips" of Glorantha.

How it works: Roll your Folk Magic skill (or Rune Affinity in AiG). Costs 1 MP. Duration = your POW in minutes. Intensity = 1 (fixed).
  • Bladesharp — +1 damage to a weapon for POW minutes
  • Heal — restore 1d3 HP to one location
  • Protection — +1 AP to all locations
  • Fanaticism — ignore wound penalties for the duration
  • Disruption — deal 1d3 damage to a random location (ranged)

Everyone should master Folk Magic before moving to their cult's system.

3

Rune Affinities (AiG)

AiG p.59–60 · ADR-002

All Systems In Adventures in Glorantha, individual casting skills are replaced by Rune Affinities.

Three Runes per character: Primary (POW+CHA+10), Secondary (POW+CHA), Tertiary (POW+CHA-10). These are your casting skills for everything.
Casting "Bladesharp" (a Death-aspected spell): → Roll your Death Rune affinity → If Death is your Primary: base 54% → If Death is your Tertiary: base 34%
  • Each spell/miracle is associated with one or more Runes
  • You roll the highest applicable Rune you possess
  • Spells with "Any" rune = use your best Rune affinity
Choose your three Runes to match your cult's magic. An Orlanth worshipper wants Air, Movement, and one of Storm/Death/Truth.
4

Theism / Rune Magic

Core p.179–196 · AiG p.69–122

Theism The most common system in Glorantha. Worshippers channel their god's power through miracles.

Devotional Pool = POW ÷ 2. This is how many miracles you can have "prepared" at once. You choose which miracles to fill your pool with during worship.
  • Casting: Roll Rune Affinity. Cost = 1+ MP depending on miracle.
  • Intensity: Rune Affinity ÷ 10 (round down). Higher affinity = more powerful miracles.
  • Miracles are gifts from your god — you can only use miracles your cult grants.
  • Exhort: Spend extra MP to boost a miracle beyond normal intensity.
Orlanth initiate with Air 54%, Devotional Pool 6: → Can prepare 6 miracles (from Orlanth's list of 53) → Casts "Lightning" at Intensity 5 (54÷10) → Rolls Air Rune to cast
5

Animism / Spirit Magic

Core p.131–154 · AiG p.134–151

Animism Spirit worshippers bind spirits into fetishes and command them to produce magical effects.

Bound Spirit Slots = CHA ÷ 2. Each slot holds one spirit in a fetish (a physical object housing the spirit).
  • Casting: Roll Spirit Rune affinity to command a bound spirit. The spirit does the magic.
  • Spirits have abilities — a Healing Spirit can heal, a Fire Spirit can burn, etc.
  • Spirit Combat: To bind a spirit, you must defeat it in spirit combat (opposed Willpower).
  • Fetishes: Physical objects (bones, carved stones, pouches) that house bound spirits.
Daka Fal shaman with CHA 12, Spirit Rune 58%: → 6 bound spirit slots → Starts with 2-3 intensity 1-2 spirits in fetishes → Commands "Ancestor Spirit" by rolling Spirit Rune → Spirit provides guidance, healing, or protection
Hannu's house rule: Binding skill is replaced by Spirit Rune affinity. You don't need a separate Binding skill.
6

Spirit Combat

Core p.134–136

Animism When you encounter hostile spirits or try to bind new ones, you enter spirit combat.

Spirit Combat: Opposed rolls of Willpower vs. Spirit's Combat skill. Damage reduces Magic Points (not HP). At 0 MP, you lose — the spirit can possess you or flee.
  • Discorporation: Shamans can leave their body to enter the Spirit World and seek spirits.
  • Spirit Abilities: Bound spirits can use Covert, Manifest, Possess, or their specific powers.
  • Intensity: A spirit's power level. Higher intensity = more MP, stronger abilities.
7

Sorcery

Core p.162–178

Sorcery Intellectual magic. Sorcerers manipulate reality through formulae and willpower, shaping spells with four principles.

Two skills: Invocation (Rune Affinity of the spell) to cast, Shaping (Law Rune) to modify. Each Shaping point adjusts Duration, Range, Targets, or Magnitude.
  • Casting: Roll Rune Affinity of the spell. Cost = 1 MP per Shaping point used.
  • Shaping: Spend points to extend duration, increase range, add targets, or boost magnitude.
  • No prepared slots — sorcerers can cast any spell they know, but shaping costs MP.
  • Grimoires: Spell books. You learn spells from your school's grimoire.
Arkat sorcerer with Law Rune 46%: → Shaping capacity = 4 (46÷10) → Casts "Damage Resistance" shaped for: Duration +2, Range +1, Magnitude +1 = 4 shaping points, 4 MP
8

Mysticism

Core p.155–161

Mysticism Internal magic. Mystics enhance themselves through meditation and self-discipline. No external power source.

No MP cost. Mystic talents are powered by Concentration — maintaining focus. You can sustain talents = Meditation skill ÷ 10.
  • Paths: Each mystic follows a Path (e.g., Path of the Warrior, Path of the Sage) that grants specific Talents.
  • Talents: Self-enhancement abilities — boost attributes, gain supernatural senses, resist magic.
  • Limitation: Talents only affect the mystic themselves. No healing others, no offensive magic at range.
  • Consequence: Using mysticism while emotionally agitated causes it to fail.
Mysticism is rare in Glorantha — primarily found in Kralori and some Eastern traditions. Your campaign may not use it.
9

Combining Systems

Core p.121 · AiG p.59–62

All Systems Many cults are hybrid — combining two magic systems. This is common in Glorantha.

Hybrid cults: Waha and Storm Bull are theist+animist. Their members get both a Devotional Pool AND Bound Spirit Slots. They can use miracles AND command spirits.
  • Stacking: You can have Folk Magic + your cult's system active simultaneously.
  • No mixing within a round: You can only cast from one system per action (can't invoke a miracle and command a spirit in the same turn).
  • Folk Magic is always available — every character has it regardless of cult system.
Waha khan (theist+animist): → Turn 1: Cast "Bladesharp" (Folk Magic, 1 MP) → Turn 2: Invoke "Command Herd Beast" (Miracle, Devotional Pool) → Turn 3: Command bound Healing Spirit (Animism, Spirit Rune)
10

Magic in Combat

Core p.92–93

All Systems Using magic in combat costs Action Points and follows specific rules:

  • Cast Magic (Proactive, 1 AP): Cast a spell on your turn. Takes 1 Turn to complete.
  • Hold Magic (Free): Prepare a spell to release later as a Reactive action.
  • Counter Spell (Reactive, 1 AP): Attempt to neutralize an incoming spell.
  • Spoil Spell (Special Effect): If you hit a caster mid-cast, their spell fails.
Concentration spells require ongoing focus. If you take damage while concentrating, roll Willpower or lose the spell.
Pre-buff before combat! Cast Bladesharp, Protection, and Fanaticism before initiative is rolled. In-combat casting costs precious AP.
11

Enchantments & Permanent Magic

Core p.121

All Systems Creating lasting magical effects requires sacrifice:

  • Enchantments: Permanently imbue an object with a spell. Costs permanent POW sacrifice.
  • Matrices: Store a spell in an object for later one-use activation. Cheaper than enchantments.
  • Bound spirits in items: Animists bind spirits into weapons/armour for permanent magical properties.
  • Temple enchantments: Theist temples can create sacred items through ritual worship.
POW sacrifice: Creating permanent magic costs permanent POW (reducing your Magic Points maximum). This is why magical items are rare and precious.
