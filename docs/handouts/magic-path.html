1

Learn the four sheet terms first

Adventures in Glorantha p.59-60; Mythras Core Rules p.131-154 and p.162-178

Rune Affinity is your percentage for a Gloranthan rune, such as Air, Earth, Death, Spirit, or Law. Adventures in Glorantha introduces rune-based magic and affinities; our table uses them to make Gloranthan magic feel different from default Mythras skill names.

Spirit Rune is the Rune Affinity used when an animist binds or commands a spirit at this table. Trance is the default roll when the question is whether you sense, enter, follow, or navigate the spirit world; use another skill only when the GM says the scene is really mundane perception, social negotiation, research, or another non-spirit-world task.

Invocation is the sorcery skill from the Mythras Core Rules for casting a sorcery spell. Sorcery spells in this campaign do not have assigned rune affinities, even when they come from a Zzistori background.

Shaping is the sorcery skill from the Mythras Core Rules for changing a spell's range, duration, targets, magnitude, or other parameters.

Mysticism is the mystic discipline from the Mythras Core Rules. It is not cult Rune Magic, spirit binding, or sorcery; read it as its own tradition.

3

Folk Magic is the shared starter layer

Adventures in Glorantha p.63-68

Most characters know a few practical spells. Before play, write each folk spell's effect, cost, duration, and whether you expect to cast it before or during danger.

  • Good first reads: Bladesharp, Protection, Heal, Mobility, Fanaticism, and Coordination.
  • Do not try to memorize the whole list. Know your own spells first.
4

Cult Rune Magic: miracles come from cult commitment

Adventures in Glorantha p.69-122; cult one-pagers

Characters with cult Rune Magic learn miracles through cults. Bring the miracle name, rune, category, cost, and recovery rule to the table.

  • Any Rune or normal cult miracles: learned through your cult and recovered through worship, sacrifice, and access to an appropriate shrine, temple, or holy place.
  • Subservient cult miracles: tied to a related subcult or servant cult; expect temple access and cult obligation to matter.
  • Associated cult miracles: one-use until you return to the associated cult and make the needed offering again.
5

Animism: spirits are helpers with abilities

Mythras Core Rules p.131-154; A Bird in the Hand p.43-46; Monster Island only for GM-assigned island exceptions

Animists bind spirits into fetishes and command them for help. A spirit is not just a spell name: it has intensity, characteristics, abilities, and limits. A Shaman career gives you independent career spirit magic; Waha gives cult spirit magic alongside cult Rune Magic.

  • Write each spirit's ability in plain language: heal, bless, scout, guard, fight spirits, or reveal hidden things.
  • Use Trance first when you are trying to notice, reach, track, or move through the spirit world. After a spirit is present and the question is control, use Spirit Rune to bind or command it.
  • Track your bound spirit slots. A starting Spirit Worshipper uses CHA/2 slots.
  • Read examples in A-Bird-in-the-Hand.pdf p.43-47. If the GM says a Monster Island exception applies, ask for the exact page and table ruling.
Monster Island table note: Monster Island can add harsher island-specific magic ecology, ritual timing, spirit access, and special substances, but those exceptions are GM-controlled notes, not character-generator rules. If the GM uses one, write the exact ruling and page they give you next to the affected spell, spirit, or miracle.
6

Sorcery: prepare simple versions before play

Mythras Core Rules p.162-178

Sorcery is flexible, but that flexibility can slow the table. A Sorcerer career and a Zzistori background both use the same core skills: Invocation to cast and Shaping to modify. For each known spell, write one fast version and one shaped version, and note the Invocation and Shaping percentages on your sheet.

  • Fast version: the minimum casting you can use under pressure.
  • Shaped version: the same spell with planned changes to range, duration, targets, or magnitude, using your Shaping skill.
  • Monster Island: if the campaign is using island-specific magic exceptions, ask the GM before assuming you can rush a shaped spell.
6b

Mysticism: use the book's discipline

Mythras Core Rules p.154-161

Mysticism is not a cult spell list. If your sheet says Mystic, read only the talents you selected, your Mysticism skill, the Magic Point cost if any, and whether a benefit is passive or must be activated. Treat a talent as passive only when its text describes an always-on discipline or ongoing benefit; treat it as active when it calls for a roll, Magic Points, concentration, activation, or a specific moment in the scene. When unsure, bring the Mythras Core Rules p.154-161 and ask the GM before the first scene where the talent matters.
