Conflict rule: for the campaign differences listed below, this handout overrides the books. If something is not listed here, use the book.

1b. Career magic and cult magic are different providers

Character Generator; Mythras Core p.131-178; Adventures in Glorantha p.69-122

Some higher magic comes from a career, some from a cult or source path, and some characters can have both. The generator should show only the current providers after you change culture, career, cult, or source choices.

If your sheet says...Treat it as...
Shaman with no cultCareer-backed Animism using Spirit Rune and Trance.
Waha cultistHybrid cult magic: Rune Magic from Waha plus cult-backed Animism. This does not make your career Shaman unless you chose that career.
Sorcerer with no cultCareer-backed Sorcery using Invocation and Shaping.
Zzistori/source-backed sorcererSource-backed Sorcery using the same Invocation and Shaping rules.
MysticCareer-backed Mysticism using RAW Mythras Mysticism.

2. Rune Magic uses Rune Affinity

Adventures in Glorantha p.59-60 and p.69-122

When you cast a miracle, choose the rune named by the miracle or cult, roll that Rune Affinity, and spend from your Devotional Pool. You do not need to learn a separate casting skill for miracles in this campaign.

RankDevotional PoolWhat this means
InitiatePOW/2Most starting theists. Long-duration miracles can tie up this pool until they end.
Acolyte, when a cult uses that rankThree quarters of POWA deeper temple role with more magic held in service.
PriestPOWFull cult service and the largest pool.

3. Miracle learning, use, and recovery

Adventures in Glorantha p.69-122; cult one-pagers

Miracle categoryHow to treat it in play
Any Rune or normal cult miracleYou learn it through your cult. You recover the committed pool through worship, sacrifice, and access to an appropriate shrine, temple, or holy place.
Subservient cult miracleIt comes through a related servant or subcult. Expect temple access, annual obligations, and local cult relationships to matter.
Associated cult miracleIt is a special one-use gift until you return to the associated cult and make the needed offering again. Mark it clearly on your sheet.

4. Animism uses Spirit Rune for binding and commanding

Mythras Core p.131-154; Monster-Island.pdf p.129-140 and p.168; A Bird in the Hand p.43-47

  • Roll Spirit Rune when binding or commanding a spirit at this table.
  • Use Trance when the scene is about sensing, entering, or navigating the spirit world.
  • A starting Spirit Worshipper has CHA/2 bound spirit slots.
  • A spirit must have a clear ability written on your sheet, not just a name.
  • Monster Island adds island-specific table rules: slow Magic Point recovery of one per day, rank-gated friendly/neutral/hostile spirit access for kahunas, combined-SIZ Dominate Species for Nature Spirits, and substances such as Kahuna's Bane that can block access to the Spirit World.
  • The character generator does not enforce Monster Island recovery, ritual time penalties, pacts, or poisons. Bring the page reference when those matter in play.

5. Sorcery uses Invocation and Shaping

Mythras Core p.162-178

  • Roll Invocation to cast a sorcery spell.
  • Roll Shaping to change range, duration, targets, magnitude, or similar spell parameters.
  • Sorcery spells do not have assigned rune affinities in this campaign.
  • Starting dedicated sorcerers know three spells.
  • Before game day, write one fast version and one shaped version of each spell.
  • Zzistori/source-backed sorcery still uses Invocation and Shaping.
  • On Monster Island, Sorcery and Theism use longer ritual casting assumptions; rushed casting uses the Casting Time Difficulty table.

5b. Mysticism uses RAW Mythras Mysticism

Mythras Core p.154-161

  • Roll Mysticism only when a selected talent or the scene calls for it.
  • Mysticism is not a cult miracle list, not spirit binding, and not sorcery.
  • Track which talents are passive and which need activation or Magic Points.

6. What to bring when a rule is unclear

  • The name of the book PDF and page number you checked.
  • The name of your cult one-pager if the question is cult-specific.
  • Your character sheet with the relevant rune, skill, pool, or spirit noted.
