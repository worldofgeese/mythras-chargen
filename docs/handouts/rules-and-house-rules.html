Player source order

Conflict rule: for the campaign differences listed below, this handout overrides the books. If something is not listed here, use the book.

1b. Specialist magic can come from different places

Character Generator; Mythras rulebook p.131-178; Adventures in Glorantha p.69-122

Some specialist magic comes from a career, some from a cult, some from a culture/background such as Zzistori, and some characters can have more than one source of magic. The generator should update the available spells, spirits, miracles, and skills after you change culture, career, cult, or background choices.

When the generator asks for a specialty, choose the concrete sheet entry rather than leaving the category label. For example, use Invocation (Zzistori School), Mysticism (Core Mysticism Path), Musicianship (Drums), Navigation (Sartar), or a named Combat Style. Subject-bearing Passions also need their subject, such as Loyalty (Clan) or Love (Family).

If your sheet says...Treat it as...
Shaman with no cultSpirit magic from your career, using Spirit Rune and Trance.
Waha cultistCombined cult magic: Rune Magic from Waha plus spirit magic from Waha. This does not make your career Shaman unless you chose that career.
Sorcerer with no cultSorcery from your career, using Invocation and Shaping.
Zzistori sorcererSorcery from your Zzistori culture/background, using the same Invocation and Shaping rules.
MysticMysticism from your career, using the mystic talent rules in the Mythras rulebook.

2. Rune Magic uses Rune Affinity

Adventures in Glorantha p.59-60 and p.69-122

When you cast a miracle, choose the rune named by the miracle or cult, roll that Rune Affinity, and spend from your Devotional Pool. You do not need to learn a separate casting skill for miracles in this campaign.

RankDevotional PoolWhat this means
InitiatePOW/2Most starting cult Rune Magic characters. Long-duration miracles can tie up this pool until they end.

Adventures in Glorantha p.69-122; cult one-pagers

Miracle categoryHow to treat it in play

Subservient cult miracleIt comes through a related servant or subcult. Expect temple access, annual obligations, and local cult relationships to matter.
Associated cult miracleIt is a special one-use gift until you return to the associated cult and make the needed offering again. Mark it clearly on your sheet.

4. Animism uses Spirit Rune for binding and commanding

Mythras rulebook p.131-154; A Bird in the Hand p.43-47; Monster Island only for GM-assigned island exceptions

  • Use Trance first when the question is whether you sense, enter, follow, or navigate the spirit world.
  • Roll Spirit Rune when binding or commanding a spirit at this table, after a spirit is present and the question is control.
  • A starting Spirit Worshipper has CHA/2 bound spirit slots.
  • A spirit must have a clear ability written on your sheet, not just a name.
  • Monster Island can add island-specific table rules, but those are GM-provided exceptions for this campaign rather than character-generator rules.
  • The character generator does not enforce Monster Island recovery, ritual time penalties, pacts, or poisons. Bring the exact GM-approved page and ruling when those matter in play.

5. Sorcery uses Invocation and Shaping

Mythras rulebook p.162-178

  • Roll Invocation to cast a sorcery spell.
  • Roll Shaping to change range, duration, targets, magnitude, or similar spell parameters.
  • Sorcery spells do not have assigned rune affinities in this campaign.
  • Starting dedicated sorcerers know three spells.
  • Before game day, write one fast version and one shaped version of each spell.
  • Zzistori sorcery still uses Invocation and Shaping.
  • If the GM uses Monster Island casting exceptions, ask before assuming you can rush Sorcery or cult Rune Magic.

5b. Mysticism uses the Mythras rulebook's mystic talent rules

Mythras rulebook p.154-161

  • Roll Mysticism only when a selected talent calls for a roll, Magic Points, concentration, activation, or a specific moment in the scene.
  • Mysticism is not a cult miracle list, not spirit binding, and not sorcery.
  • Treat a talent as passive only when its text describes an always-on discipline or ongoing benefit. If the wording is unclear, bring the Mythras rulebook p.154-161 and ask the GM before it matters in play.

6. What to bring when a rule is unclear

  • The name of the book PDF and page number you checked.
  • The name of your cult one-pager if the question is cult-specific.
  • Your character sheet with the relevant rune, skill, pool, or spirit noted.
