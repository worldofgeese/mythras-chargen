Welcome to RuneQuest

You’re adventurers in Glorantha: a myth-filled bronze-age world where gods are real, your cult matters, and your loyalties shape who you become. You’ve arrived in war-scarred Jonstown looking for shelter, work, and the next right thing to do together. Starter Set Book 4 pp. 5-8

Gods are active. Cults grant magic, duties, and identity. Runes show the powers that pull on your life. Starter Set Book 1 pp. 18-22
Violence matters. Combat is dangerous, fast, and full of lasting consequences. Winning cleanly matters. Book 4 p. 5; Mythras Core pp. 86-100
You already belong. Your group starts as allies and traveling companions, not strangers in a tavern. Starter Set Book 4 p. 5

The Opening Situation

Dark Season. Jonstown is still recovering from war. Mercenaries, refugees, and hungry opportunists crowd the streets. You’ve made it through the North Gate at dusk and headed for the North Market hoping to find food, beds, and maybe paid work.

What you know. The Battle of Dangerford was recent. The Lunar Empire has been driven out, but no one thinks the land is safe yet. Starter Set Book 4 p. 6
What matters. Passions like Honor, Loyalty, Hate, and Devotion are not decoration. They guide choices and can help you act decisively. Starter Set Book 4 p. 5; Starter Set Book 1 pp. 17-19
What this first adventure feels like. Street trouble, hard choices, wounded pride, social pressure, and a city that hasn’t decided whether to trust you yet. Starter Set Book 4 pp. 5-10

How To Play

1
Roll d100 and try to get equal to or under the skill on your sheet. You’ll most often roll a skill, a Passion, a rune affinity, or a combat style. Mythras Core pp. 37-46
2
If someone opposes you, the better success wins. That’s true in arguments, chases, and fights. A better quality of success beats a worse one. Mythras Core pp. 37-46
3
In combat, you act with Action Points. Attack, parry, evade, react, and press advantages by spending Action Points at the right moment. Mythras Core pp. 86-100
4
Use Passions and Runes when the fiction supports them. If your Honor, Loyalty, Devotion, or rune nature clearly applies, bring it in. That’s how heroes feel larger than life. Starter Set Book 1 pp. 17-22
5
Magic comes from your cult and your runes. Folk magic is broad and practical. Miracles key off the right rune affinity and your Devotion. Mythras Core pp. 179-182; Adventures in Glorantha pp. 71-74

One Exchange In Combat

The first adventure opens with trouble in the North Market, so this is the sort of thing you’ll do right away. Starter Set Book 4 pp. 7-10

Vasana rides in. She spends 1 Action Point to attack with Vinga Cavalry, the combat style that covers her weapons and mounted fighting. Mythras Core pp. 86-90
The troll reacts. It spends 1 Action Point to parry. Both sides roll. The better result wins the exchange. Mythras Core pp. 90-96
If Vasana wins cleanly, damage lands and a Special Effect may follow. That might mean forcing a stumble, choosing a location, or opening the enemy up for the next ally. Mythras Core pp. 96-100
If she calls on a miracle, the right rune does the casting. Her cult and rune affinities matter in play, not just as backstory. Adventures in Glorantha pp. 71-74
