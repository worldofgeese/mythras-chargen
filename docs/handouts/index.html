Read in this order

  1. Create your character. Open the Character Generator, follow the steps, then export a PDF sheet.
  2. Learn the basic dice language. Read Mythras Core Rulebook p.20-23 for characteristics and skill base values, then p.38-42 for skill tests and opposed rolls.
  3. Learn your homeland and culture. Read Adventures in Glorantha p.23-41 for cultures, careers, and combat styles that match your sheet.
  4. Learn the first combat loop. Read Combat Path stages 1-4, then Mythras Core Rulebook p.74-106 when you want the full procedure.
  5. Learn only your own magic first. Everyone with spells reads Folk Magic in Adventures in Glorantha p.63-68. Then read the section in Magic Path for your own tradition: cult Rune Magic, Shaman Animism, Sorcerer or Zzistori Sorcery, Mystic Mysticism, or Waha's hybrid path.
  6. Read the table differences. Read House Rules before using miracles, spirits, sorcery, devotional pools, or cult recovery rules.
  7. Use the session checklist. Before game day, finish the Prep Checklist.

If you are mostly a warrior

Know your combat style, the weapons inside it, Action Points, armour, hit locations, and two Special Effects you are likely to use.

If you are a cult initiate

Know your Rune Affinities, selected miracles, Devotional Pool, and how each miracle is recovered after use.

If you work with spirits

Know whether your spirits come from a Shaman career or a cult such as Waha, your Spirit Rune value, bound spirit slots, each spirit's ability, and whether the scene needs spirit-world perception or a binding/command roll.

If you are a sorcerer

Know whether your sorcery comes from a Sorcerer career or a source-backed path such as Zzistori, your three spells, your Invocation and Shaping percentages, and one fast version plus one shaped version of each spell.

If you are a mystic

Know your Mysticism skill, selected talents, and which benefits are always-on discipline versus active techniques that need a roll or Magic Points.
