The Combat Path

Read this before your first fight. It teaches the table workflow first, then points to the book pages for the full rules.

1

Combat is an opposed test

Mythras rulebook p.74-106

You usually roll an attack against a defender's parry, evade, or other response. Compare success levels first; then resolve damage and Special Effects.

At the table: say what you do, roll the relevant combat style, compare success levels, choose any Special Effect, then mark damage on a location.
2

Action Points are your round budget

Mythras rulebook p.74-76

Attacking, parrying, evading, casting, and other major actions spend from a small pool. If you run out, you are much easier to hurt.

  • Do not spend your last Action Point casually.
  • Multiple enemies are dangerous because they drain reactions.
  • Casters need allies because casting competes with defending.
3

Combat Style is the skill; weapons are permissions

Mythras rulebook p.49-50; Adventures in Glorantha p.25-41

Your combat style percentage applies to the weapons listed inside that style. A sling, sword, spear, or shield should not be rolled at 0% if it belongs to a style you know.

Before play: write which carried weapons belong to each style and copy the style percentage beside those weapons.
4

Special Effects are your tactical choices

Mythras rulebook p.94-106

Special Effects often matter more than raw damage. Pick a few that fit your character before the first fight.

  • Choose Location targets a weak or unarmoured location.
  • Trip Opponent controls an enemy's next action.
  • Disarm can end a fight without killing.
  • Impale and Bleed make wounds spiral.
5

Hit locations make armour specific

Mythras rulebook p.78-83

Damage lands on a location. Armour protects only that location, and a wounded arm remains a problem even if your chest is healthy.

Player habit: mark damage on the exact location immediately, then check whether the wound changes what you can do.
6

Choose a risk posture before danger

Mythras rulebook p.92-93

Before the first roll, decide whether the party is ambushing, holding ground, withdrawing, protecting a caster, or trying to disable rather than kill.
