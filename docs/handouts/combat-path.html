The Combat Path

Read this before your first fight. It teaches Mythras combat in the order you will need it at the table.

1

Combat is an opposed test, not an attack roll race

MYT p.74-106; AiG combat styles

You usually roll an attack against a defender's parry, evade, or other defensive response. The better success level wins.

At the table: announce your action, roll the relevant combat style, compare success levels, then resolve damage and any Special Effects.
Primary rule: Mythras Core combat procedure. Glorantha adaptation: AiG combat styles and culture-specific fighting traditions. Chargen data: combat styles and weapons in index.html.
2

Action Points are your round budget

MYT p.74-76; character sheet attributes

Each attack, parry, evade, spell cast, or other major combat action spends from a small pool. Running out means you cannot defend normally.

  • Do not spend your last Action Point casually.
  • If you cast in melee, you may be unable to parry later in the cycle.
  • Multiple opponents are dangerous because they drain your reactions.
Example: A warrior with 2 AP attacks once and saves one AP to parry. A reckless warrior attacks twice and has no parry left.
3

Combat Style is the skill, weapons are permissions

AiG p.25-41 culture styles; Mythras Core p.49-50 combat styles; Source Trail: combat-style data

Your combat style percentage applies to the weapons listed inside that style. If your Balazaring Hunter style includes Sling, the Sling row should use that style value.

Check before play: know which weapons belong to each of your combat styles and write the same percentage beside those weapons.
Implementation note: Play Mode and PDF export derive weapon percentages from the matching combat style so a weapon such as Sling does not show 0% when its style is known.
4

Special Effects are the reason to fight tactically

MYT p.94-106

When you win the opposed roll by the required margin, choose a Special Effect. They often matter more than raw damage.

  • Choose Location targets an unarmoured or wounded spot.
  • Trip Opponent controls the enemy's next action.
  • Disarm or Damage Weapon can end a fight without killing.
  • Impale, Bleed, and similar effects make wounds spiral.
5

Hit locations make armour and positioning matter

MYT p.78-83; character sheet HP table

Damage lands on a location. Armour protects that location, and wounds apply penalties or incapacitation depending on severity.

Player habit: track each location separately. A healthy chest does not save a ruined arm.
6

Before combat, choose risk posture

MYT p.92-93; Magic in Combat stages 1-3

Decide whether you are ambushing, holding ground, withdrawing, protecting a caster, or trying to disable rather than kill.

  • Buff before initiative if the fiction gives you time.
  • Keep allies between enemies and casters.
  • Use terrain to avoid being surrounded.
